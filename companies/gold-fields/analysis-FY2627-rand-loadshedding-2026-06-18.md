## Rand weakness & load-shedding — FY2026e / FY2027e scenario & sensitivity analysis

_Internal IC briefing. All FY2025 figures are reported actuals (Gold Fields FY2025 reviewed results, SEC Form 6-K, 19–20 Feb 2026, verified vs the official results microsite). All forward figures are analyst estimates, marked (e), with the working shown. Numbers are sacred._


## Verdict

> **Verdict (FY2026e / FY2027e).** Gold Fields is structurally insulated from both shocks because it REPORTS and SELLS in US dollars and runs only ONE South African mine — South Deep, ~14% of group ounces. (A) A substantial rand depreciation is, in US-dollar terms, a MILD POSITIVE: group revenue is unchanged (USD-priced gold) while South Deep's ZAR cost base translates to fewer dollars, easing group AISC — we model +US$100m EBITDA FY2026e and +US$107m FY2027e (e), ~+1.4%. For a rand investor it is clearly positive: rand-translated EBITDA and dividends rise ~+29% (e). (B) Renewed Stage 4–6 load-shedding is a contained South Deep headwind — ~−US$74m EBITDA FY2026e and ~−US$76m FY2027e (e), ~−1.0%. COMBINED, the weak-rand cost tailwind roughly offsets the load-shedding drag — a near wash in USD (+US$26m FY2026e / +US$31m FY2027e (e)) but ~+29% in ZAR. Both shocks are dwarfed by the US-dollar gold price: a single +US$100/oz move (+US$250m EBITDA (e)) outweighs either SA shock in full.


## 1.  Why both shocks are localised — the structural nuance

Gold Fields is a US-dollar reporter that sells gold at a US-dollar price, so group revenue does not change when the rand moves. The rand and the Eskom grid enter the group P&L through one asset only — South Deep, the sole South African mine (~90.2% owned), ~14% of group attributable ounces (~320koz of 2.438Moz FY2025, ramping toward a ~380koz/a run-rate). The other ~85% sits in Australia (AUD costs), Ghana, Peru, Chile and — pre-build — Canada, none of which touch the rand or Eskom. South Deep is also the only Gold Fields asset on the Eskom grid. So both shocks are ring-fenced to South Deep, and group-level USD sensitivity is muted versus a pure-SA miner.

- Weaker rand → South Deep cost channel (tailwind). South Deep's ZAR-denominated costs translate into fewer US dollars, so group AISC eases and South Deep's USD margin widens. Per the mining lens, always separate the price effect (nil here — USD gold) from the FX effect (the classic 'rand hedge' on the cost side).
- Load-shedding → South Deep cost + volume channel (headwind). Underground hoisting, ventilation, pumping and refrigeration run 24/7; outages force diesel self-generation (higher cost) and can stop hoisting (lost ounces). Eskom is the classic SA operational swing factor in the mining lens.

## 3.  Scenario A — Substantial rand weakness (USD/ZAR ~21–22)

The mechanism. Group revenue is set by US-dollar gold price × ounces sold and is unaffected by the ZAR. The rand enters only through South Deep's cost base. A ~25–35% depreciation (to ~R21–22 from ~R16.2 spot) trims group AISC by roughly US$30–60/oz (e) — we model −US$40/oz — lifting group EBITDA +US$100m FY2026e and +US$107m FY2027e (e), about +1.4% on the modelled base. Real, but second-order.

What matters for a rand investor. The JSE share price and the dividend are denominated in rand. With US-dollar earnings unchanged, a ~25–30% weaker rand mechanically lifts the rand share price and the rand dividend by a similar amount — modelled rand EBITDA rises from R121bn to ~R156bn FY2026e (+29%) and to ~R173bn FY2027e (e). On flat USD payout, the FY2025 R25.50 ordinary dividend would translate ~25–30% higher purely on FX. Gold Fields is a natural rand hedge for a rand-based holder.


## 4.  Scenario B — Load-shedding reintroduced (Stage 4–6)

Starting point. Load-shedding has been suspended 341+ consecutive days and Eskom forecasts none for winter 2026 (S5) — so this is a re-introduction stress, not the base case. Exposure is ring-fenced to South Deep, the only Gold Fields asset on the Eskom grid.

Mitigation is already built. South Deep's 50MW Khanyisa solar plant generates at ~8.5% of Eskom grid cost and at times exports surplus; a solar-plus-battery and a wind study are under evaluation (S3). Solar is daytime-only and cannot cover the 24/7 underground load, so it cushions rather than removes the risk.

Size of the effect. A return to Stage 4–6 plausibly costs South Deep ~5–15koz and adds ~US$50–150/oz to its AISC; weighted to ~14% of the group that is ~US$20/oz on group AISC and ~5koz on group output — a group EBITDA drag of ~−US$74m FY2026e and ~−US$76m FY2027e (e), ~−1.0% of the modelled base (within the ~US$50–120m envelope).

## 6.  What actually moves the needle — the gold price

- Gold price: ±US$100/oz → ±US$250m EBITDA (e) at ~2.5Moz. Gold itself swung from US$4,152 (10 Jun) to US$4,301/oz (18 Jun 2026) (S4) — a ~US$150/oz move in eight days, ~US$375m of annualised EBITDA (e), larger than the full-year impact of either SA scenario.
- Group AISC: ±US$100/oz → ∓US$250m EBITDA (e). The rand and load-shedding both work through AISC, but their net swing (±~US$40–60/oz) is small next to a gold-price move.
- Implication. A Gold Fields position is a bet on the US-dollar gold price and on delivery against guidance — not on the rand or Eskom. The SA factors are a modest, mostly favourable, swing around that. (House judgement.)


## 7.  Risk flags & watch items (mining lens)

- Gold-price reversal — the real risk. Gold is up ~28% y/y; a sharp pullback hurts far more than any SA shock. A Fed turn toward hikes is a real-rate headwind to watch (S4).
- South Deep ramp execution. The 320→380koz/a step-up is the SA value driver; load-shedding or grid instability would slow it. Track Khanyisa output and the battery/wind FID (S2/S3).
- Non-SA portfolio events dwarf the SA levers. Tarkwa (Ghana) mining leases expire April 2027 (renewal not automatic); Damang handed back to the State April 2026; Windfall (Canada) FID guided mid-2026. Each is a larger swing factor than load-shedding (S1).
- Rand STRENGTH, not weakness, is the mild negative. The scenario asked about is favourable-to-neutral; the less-discussed risk is a stronger rand lifting South Deep's USD cost and deflating rand-translated returns.
- Cost inflation. A weak rand usually rides on higher SA inflation and SARB rates (repo 7.00% after the 28 May 2026 hike), which over time lifts South Deep's rand wage and consumable costs, eroding part of the FX cost benefit (S4).

- Stakeholder outcomes. The Thusano employee share trust holds ~10.75% of GFIMSA (the SA holdco), paying employee dividends; South Deep Education & Community Trusts fund jobs, adult education, infrastructure and small-enterprise development. The same SA shocks modelled financially also pressure host communities, raising the value of these programmes (S3).
- AllWeather view: climate ambition is sound and the renewables build genuine, but thin 2024 progress vs the 2016 baseline is the item to scrutinise; the trust structures are a tangible, differentiated positive.
