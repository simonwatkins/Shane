"THE FINANCE GHOST: The Clicks conundrum — Will Dis-Chem sneak up from behind?"
By The Finance Ghost (contributor). 30 April 2026.

Captured via web fetch on 2026-06-07. Summary of key points (full text behind paywall):

CORE ARGUMENT: Clicks trades at 52-week lows after H1 FY2026 results. The business is not broken
(24.9% retail pharmacy share; H1 diluted HEPS +8.1%, well above inflation) but the SHARE is
struggling.
- 3-year price return just 4.7% (cumulative, not annualised); +14.4% total return incl. dividends
  — less than fixed income would have delivered over the period.
- Valuation: Clicks has "always been expensive"; the issue is slow growth at a high-20s P/E.
- Quality of the H1 number: headline EARNINGS grew only 6.4%; the gap up to 8.1% HEPS growth was
  closed by share buybacks. FY2026 guidance of 4%-9% diluted HEPS growth is "particularly weak."
- In a market with strong bond yields, slow-growing equity stories can't support high multiples.
- Rotation thesis: investors moving from "safety and quality" toward riskier local names enjoying
  an upswing in SA conditions, making Clicks relatively less appealing.

COMPETITIVE FRAMING (the key insight for coverage):
- Invokes Hemingway's "gradually, then suddenly" — the Shoprite vs Pick n Pay divergence as a
  warning. Asks whether the same could happen in pharmacy / health & beauty.
- Dis-Chem (JSE: DCP; CEO Rui Morais) is "telling an encouraging story": trading update 1 Sep 2025
  -16 Feb 2026 showed group revenue +10.1% (retail +9.5%, external wholesale +13.7%); FY (to Feb
  2026) results due 29 May 2026.
- Dis-Chem "sounds like a far more modern retailer" — talking up data, integrations, strategic
  partnerships (e.g. Capitec); "sounding like a growth company hungry for market share." Clicks,
  despite arguably SA's most iconic loyalty programme (ClubCard), "doesn't seem as exciting."
- Open question: "Will Dis-Chem vs Clicks become Shoprite vs Pick n Pay?"

CONTEXT FIGURES CITED: Clicks opened its 950th store in February and expanded its national
pharmacy footprint to 740 locations (per the article's file photo caption — note: the official
H1 FY2026 SENS states 1,003 stores / 795 pharmacies at period-end).
