"NEWS ANALYSIS: Clicks stays the course under Bertina Engelbrecht"
By Nompilo Zulu, Consumer Industries Writer. 10 February 2026.
URL: https://www.businessday.co.za/companies/company-strategy/2026-02-10-news-analysis-clicks-stays-the-course-under-bertina-engelbrecht/
Captured via web fetch on 2026-06-07. Summary of key points (full text behind paywall):

THESIS: Continuity and capital investment shape the retailer's growth under CEO Bertina
Engelbrecht (appointed Jan 2022, internal appointee with 15+ years at the group). Her tenure is
defined by steady execution, operational discipline and continuity rather than strategic reset.

SHARE PRICE / VALUATION:
- Share price +~18% since Engelbrecht became CEO, but -~10% over the trailing year.
- Market value has hovered around R75bn since 2021.
- Fund managers still view Clicks as a quality, defensive stock. MP9 Asset Management CIO Aheesh
  Singh: "a quality counter on the JSE, where the real debate is about the sustainability of that
  mid-teens compounding in a tough consumer backdrop."

STRATEGY / INVESTMENT:
- By FY2025: ~1,000 stores, materially expanded pharmacy network, more advanced digital and
  supply-chain backbone. Double-digit earnings growth; dividends in line with profits; ROE among
  highest in listed retail. Funds ~R1bn/yr capex while returning cash via dividends + buybacks.
- Capex focused on three areas: (1) store/pharmacy base expansion; (2) IT systems (new retail
  merchandising platforms, warehouse management systems, pharmacy management software); (3) supply
  chain (UPD upgrades, facilities, solar, electric delivery vehicles).
- Store formats: convenience locations, lower-income areas, UniCare 24-hour pharmacies.

CHALLENGES: Covid-related disruptions, supply-chain volatility, rising input costs, weak consumer
spending; persistent cost pressure (wages, utilities, security, logistics). UPD inventory
management an ongoing focus. Regulatory dependencies on pharmacy licensing and healthcare policy.

DIFFERENTIATORS: Private label (growing share of front-shop sales); ClubCard loyalty/data;
healthcare (expanded pharmacy services, primary care clinics, digital healthcare).

FY2026 OUTLOOK (as reported): capex >R1.2bn for further store/pharmacy openings, system rollouts,
supply-chain and energy projects; retail trading space to expand ~5%, moving toward medium-term
target of 1,200 stores.
