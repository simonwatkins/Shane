## Group highlights
- Group sales €22.4bn, +11% constant (+5% actual); Q4 +13% constant.
- Operating profit €4.5bn including €164m non-recurring costs; cost discipline mitigated weaker trading currencies and higher raw material costs.
- Continued long-term investment in craftsmanship, heritage and distribution/manufacturing footprint.

## HEPS (South African practice, per the announcement)
- Headline earnings FY2026 €3 605m (FY2025: €3 726m).
- **Basic HEPS €6.132** (FY2025: €6.351); **Diluted HEPS €6.114** (FY2025: €6.327). (Note 29 of the consolidated financial statements.)

## Business areas
- **Jewellery Maisons:** sales €16.5bn, +8% (+14% constant); operating result €5.0bn; operating margin 30.5%. Measured price increases to offset higher gold prices and FX.
- **Specialist Watchmakers:** sales €3.1bn, -4% (+1% constant); operating result €107m; operating margin 3.4%. Return to growth in H2 (A. Lange & Söhne, Jaeger-LeCoultre, Vacheron Constantin). Baume & Mercier to be sold to Damiani Group (announced 22 Jan 2026; closing summer 2026).
- **'Other' (F&A etc.):** sales €2.7bn, -2% (+3% constant); operating loss €96m. Peter Millar and Alaïa strong; Montblanc improving.

## Regions (actual / constant rates)
- Americas +8% / +17%; Middle East & Africa +6% / +13% (Q4 -3% cc, hit by March conflict); Europe +7% / +9%; Asia Pacific +1% / +8% (China/HK/Macau combined +low single digit cc); Japan +2% / +9%.
- Channels: Retail 71% of sales (+5% / +12%); Online retail (+2% / +8%); DTC 77% of sales; Wholesale 23% (+4% / +9%).

## Margins, costs, finance, tax
- Gross margin -250bps (-40bps constant): ~two-thirds from raw material (gold) cost inflation, one-third from US duties; -210bps FX impact; partly offset by pricing and mix.
- Net operating expenses 44.4% of sales (-160bps). Communication 8.9% of sales. Non-recurring costs €164m (vs €72m py): €99m impairments + €59m Baume & Mercier write-down.
- Net finance costs €144m (py €53m): FX losses on monetary items €534m, offset by €374m hedging gains; fair-value gain €109m; net interest expense €93m.
- Effective tax rate 20.4% (py 16.5%).
- Inventory €9 715m (+8%); rotation 17.1 months (py 18.6). Repaid €1.5bn 1% coupon bond in March 2026. Acquired 36% of LuxExperience BV (from YNAP sale).

## Cash flow & capital
- Operating cash flow €4 880m; free cash flow €2.8bn (+€0.6bn). Capex €1.0bn (4.6% of sales; 53% distribution / 27% manufacturing / 20% other).
- 2025 dividend of CHF 3.00 paid Sept 2025; total dividend cash outflow €1 888m. Treasury shares acquired €186m.

## Dividend
- Proposed ordinary dividend CHF 3.30 per A share/10 B (+10%) + special dividend CHF 1.00. AGM 9 September 2026.
- Withholding 35%: ordinary net CHF 2.145; special net CHF 0.65.
- JSE: cum-dividend last trade 15 Sep 2026; ex-dividend 16 Sep 2026; JSE dividend payable in ZAR on 28 Sep 2026 (SIX paid in CHF 21 Sep 2026).

## Governance / audit
- All board members re-elected (one-year terms). KPMG SA appointed auditor (one-year term), succeeding PwC.

## Chairman (Johann Rupert) — outlook
- Cautious: "uncertainty is likely to persist, not least in relation to developments in the Middle East." Group remains vigilant, relying on long-term orientation and disciplined operating approach.
