=== DEAL ===
- Announced: 9 December 2025 — binding agreement to acquire 100% of NKD Group GmbH (via target Pegasus Group Holding GmbH, which trades as the retail business of NKD).
- Target: NKD — German value fashion & homewares retailer; >2,000 stores across Germany, Austria, Italy, Croatia, Slovenia, Czech Republic and Poland. Value/discount model aligned to Mr Price's.
- Consideration: up to EUR 487m aggregate (incl. ~EUR 38.5m for shareholder loan receivables) ≈ R9.66bn.
- Conditions fulfilled 23 February 2026; transaction unconditional save for payment. Scheduled closing 31 March 2026 (post FY2026 year-end → consolidated from FY2027). Investor presentation held 17 March 2026.

=== STRATEGIC IMPACT ===
- More than doubles global store footprint to >5,000 outlets; workforce >40,000.
- Combined annual revenue projected to rise from ~R40.9bn (SA) toward ~R53bn once consolidated.
- First material offshore operating business for Mr Price → introduces significant EUR translation exposure and integration risk for a historically SA-centric, cash-generative retailer.

=== MARKET REACTION / CONTROVERSY ===
- On announcement, MRP share price fell 13.7% to R181.32, wiping ~R6bn of market value; investors "rattled".
- Mr Price publicly defended the deal amid investor concerns (17 March 2026).
- FY2026 statutory earnings were reduced by expensing all once-off NKD transaction costs (hence the gap between statutory HEPS growth ~+2% and normalised ~+8%).
