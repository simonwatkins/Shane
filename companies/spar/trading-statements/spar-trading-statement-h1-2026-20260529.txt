=== DRIVERS ===
Margin: Total continuing Group margins reflect SA margin compression, partly offset by Ireland. SA gross profit margin down 20-40 bps (elevated Black Friday promotional subsidy + KZN distribution centre underperformance). Above-inflation cost growth and elevated debtor impairments in SA Groceries & Liquor drove substantial operating profit decline. Ireland GP margin improved (better supplier terms + favourable mix); operating margin slightly ahead.
KZN: H1 weighed down by short-term strategies prioritising top-line over profit; root cause = insufficient logistics capacity planning → disrupted service levels + increased costs. Key ongoing risk, monitored at executive/Board level. Corrective measures incl. leadership changes. Three consecutive profitable months Feb/Mar/Apr 2026, but still below target.
Black Friday: strong sales but meaningful GP margin cost + elevated Nov 2025 marketing/promo costs. New Group CMO introducing disciplined promotional investment model.
Impairments: total extraordinary items ~R128m (Prior: R71m) — goodwill, corporate store and held-for-sale impairments, all within continuing ops; mostly legacy positions.
Debtor impairments: increased. SA Groceries & Liquor debtors' days stable at 32; overdue balances up. More conservative provisioning methodology → higher ECL. Year-end FY2026 provisions to be scrutinised.
Debt: net debt increased (higher working capital + Easter timing). ALL BANKING COVENANTS MET. Festive working-capital build expected to unwind Mar-Sep; debt to reduce through H2 FY2026.

=== UK ASSET DISPOSAL (AWG) ===
Per SENS 18 May 2026: asset purchase agreement with A.F. Blakemore & Son (a SPAR UK wholesaler) for the UK (AWG) business — right to operate SPAR in South-West England, 71 company-owned stores, warehouse/logistics infrastructure, retailer supply agreements. Completion staged June-September 2026. AWG remains a discontinued operation under IFRS 5.

=== LOOKING AHEAD (FY2026 H2 / into FY2027) ===
Structural initiatives to realign cost base and improve operating leverage, primarily Southern Africa:
- KZN margin recovery toward peer DC levels.
- Dedicated Managing Director appointed for SA Groceries & Liquor segment.
- Strengthening independent retailer partnerships; addressing five key retailer issues (incl. profitability, revenue growth, omnichannel).
- Cost realignment programme (non-trade procurement centralisation, org design, pricing/concession discipline, logistics productivity) — benefits accrue through H2 FY2026 and into FY2027.
- SPAR Health and Build it momentum to continue.
Headwinds for H2 FY2026: rising fuel costs (logistics/distribution), continued debtor risk / further provisioning, intensifying competition, macro uncertainty.
Cleaner balance sheet, more focused footprint, refreshed executive team; recovery will take time.
