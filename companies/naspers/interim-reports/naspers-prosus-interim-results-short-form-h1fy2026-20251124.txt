=== KEY COMMENTARY (growth rates: 1H26 vs 1H25; brackets = local-currency, ex-M&A) ===
- Consolidated revenue grew 22% (14% LC) to US$3.6bn, driven by iFood (LatAm), OLX (Europe), PayU (India).
- Ecommerce aEBITDA grew 70% (58%) to US$530m; Ecommerce aEBIT increased 97% (84%) to US$400m.
- Consolidated aEBITDA +99% (82%) from US$213m to US$423m; consolidated aEBIT from US$60m to US$250m.
- Core headline earnings +13% (18%) to US$4.0bn; core HEPS +24% (boosted by share repurchases).
- Earnings from continuing operations +23% to US$5.6bn (from US$4.6bn).
- Free cash flow US$897m -> US$1.3bn. Excluding Tencent dividend, FCF was US$59m (vs -US$104m outflow in 1H25).

=== GUIDANCE (FY2026, year to 31 March 2026) ===
- Ecommerce revenue: US$7.3bn - US$7.5bn
- Ecommerce aEBITDA: US$1.1bn - US$1.2bn (excluding JET)
- Capital Markets Day (25 June 2025): three-year plan to AT LEAST DOUBLE Ecommerce revenue and TRIPLE Ecommerce aEBITDA.

=== PORTFOLIO / CAPITAL ALLOCATION ===
- Invested US$2.0bn in M&A in 1H26 incl. Despegar (May 2025).
- OLX agreed to acquire La Centrale (France motor classifieds) for EUR1.1bn (US$1.3bn); closed Nov 2025.
- Closed Just Eat Takeaway.com (JET) acquisition Oct 2025 for ~EUR4.2bn (US$4.9bn); settled EUR5.0bn (US$5.8bn) incl. convertible bonds EUR788m (US$925m).
- Divested Udemy, part of Remitly; trimmed Meituan by US$249m in period + US$300m in October. Total divestiture proceeds (to Nov) US$1.2bn. Expect ~US$2bn divestitures in FY26.
- Balance sheet: US$20.3bn cash on hand (US$18.3bn central); net cash US$2.6bn (US$1.8bn central). After La Centrale + JET settlement: ~US$13.2bn cash on hand (US$11.2bn central). Committed to investment-grade rating.

=== TENCENT & DISCOUNT / BUYBACK ===
- Share-repurchase programme (since June 2022): reduced Prosus free-float share count by 30%; returned over US$41bn to Prosus + Naspers shareholders.
- Combined Naspers/Prosus holdco discount reduced by 25 percentage points since inception (buyback + disclosure/operational execution); US$63bn value creation through 30 Sept 2025.
- Prosus repurchased 892,713,136 ordinary N shares (US$30.1bn) -> +18% incremental NAV/share accretion.
- Naspers funds its buyback via Prosus share sales: sold 344,868,918 Prosus N shares, repurchased 60,735,037 Naspers N shares, totalling US$11.5bn.
