## 4. Relative momentum view — *informational only, not investment advice*

All statements below are synthesis from the cited documents; growth rates are each company vs its own pcp over different period lengths (see Section 1 caveat).

- **Top-line momentum currently favours Dis-Chem.** Dis-Chem grew FY2026 group revenue +9.3% with like-for-like retail sales of 5.3% (S2 p. 12; S3 p. 25), against Clicks' H1 FY2026 group turnover +7.4% with comparable-stores growth of 3.1%, decelerating from 4.6% pcp (S1, p. 1). Clicks' half was hampered by a self-inflicted, largely transitory factor — the WMS implementation delay (~R175m / 0.9% of retail sales, resolved by end-February 2026) — and festive discounting (S1, p. 2). Dis-Chem's post-year-end trading (revenue +9.0%, total income margin 32.0% for 1 Mar–19 May 2026; S3, p. 27) suggests its momentum continued into Clicks' H2 window.
- **Earnings delivery currently favours Clicks.** Clicks converted 7.4% turnover growth into +8.1% diluted HEPS growth with the trading margin held at 9.1% and ROE of 45.7% (S1, p. 1). Dis-Chem's HEPS fell 17.3% (S2, note 11, p. 35) — but on management's stated adjustments this masks an underlying improvement: HEPS −11.7% ex the prior-year property gain, group PBT +20.1% and core retail PBT +27.1% ex the R445m ecosystem spend and non-recurring items (S3, p. 25). The quality-of-earnings debate for Dis-Chem rests on whether one accepts those adjustments; the audited statutory numbers show operating margin down 110 bps (e) to 4.3% (S2, pp. 28–29).
- **Dividends diverge accordingly.** Clicks raised its interim DPS +8.4% to 258.0c at a 39.5% payout (S1, pp. 1, 3); Dis-Chem's final DPS fell 42.8% to 15.9c under its mechanical 40%-of-headline-earnings policy (S2, note 21, p. 53; S3, p. 26) — the dividend cut is a direct read-through of the earnings decline, not a policy change.
- **Both are gaining share in the same market.** Clicks' retail pharmacy market share rose to 24.9% from 24.2% (S1, p. 2); Dis-Chem claims +1.1 ppt volume market share across all core retail categories since the Better Rewards launch (S3, p. 25). Aggressive festive discounting cited by Clicks (S1, p. 2) is consistent with Dis-Chem's analytically-led promotional push — competitive intensity between the two appears to be rising.
- **What to watch:** Clicks FY2026 results (~late October 2026) against its +4% to +9% diluted-HEPS guidance (S1, p. 3); whether Dis-Chem's X, bigly labs investment delivers the "net positive returns in FY2027" management expects (S3, p. 25); and Dis-Chem's H1 FY2027 interims (~late October 2026), which will overlap Clicks' H2 FY2026 trading window.
